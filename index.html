WEEK 4 - October 3, 2016

Final Revisions

Having gained an extra week from Thanksgiving, I spent the time updating the visual comps. I realized in hindsight that there were a few sections of my site that didn't really help the narrative, such as parallax scrolling. The content also seemed a bit dry and I wanted to make it more engaging. With these realizations, I decided to remove parallax scrolling for my hero section but instead create hero section that disappears with a timer - much like Snapchat. I also used Principal to mimick the interaction design of ephemeral messages. Finally, I decided to fade out the content at the end to once again mimick the power of ephemerality.

WEEK 1 - September 12, 2016

Research

For the first class, we went over ways of categorizing information in the form of John Maeda's SLIP, and Richard Saul Wurman LATCH “hatrack”. We also talked about the arrow of disruption in technology which I found particularly intriguing with the topic I chose on Ephemeral Messaging.

“Ephemeral messaging is the mobile-to-mobile transmission of multimedia messages that automatically disappear from the recipient's screen after the message has been viewed. The word "ephemeral" describes something that only lasts for a short period of time.” TechCrunch

Ephemeral messages which "self-destruct" can be contrasted with SMS text messaging and iMessage, both of which require the recipient to physically delete messages from the device. This research reports analyzes the disruption ephemeral messaging has and continues to have on society.
